Tommy Caldwell (born August 11, 1978 in Estes Park, Colorado) is an American rock climber who competes in the sport of sport climbing. The marriage to notable American rock climber Beth Rodden, which took place in 2003, was his first marriage. Their divorce was finalised in 20In addition to sport climbing, Caldwell is proficient in a variety of traditional climbing styles, including as hard traditional climbing, big-wall speed climbing, and big-wall free climbing. At the Fortress of Solitude in Colorado, he accomplished first ascents (FA) of some of the country’s most difficult sport routes (as of 2008), including Kryptonite (5.14c/d) and Flex Luthor (perhaps a 5.15a), both of which were graded 5.14c/d. Caldwell is a big-wall climber who has made the first free ascents of a number of El Capitan climbs, among other accomplishments. In May of 2004, he made the first free climb of Dihedral Wall, which was a world record. During the summer of 2005, he and Beth Rodden (who was alternating leads with him) made the third and fourth free climbs of The Nose. Caldwell free-climbed The Nose in less than 12 hours two days later, on the 16th, on the same day. Later, Caldwell climbed The Nose in 11 hours, down the East Ledges, and then climbed Freerider, reaching the summit 12 hours later – the first ascent of two El Capitan free climbs in a single 24-hour period! Caldwell has also free-climbed the following routes on El Capitan: Lurking Fear, Muir Wall (Cosgrove var. to Magic Mushroom finish, FFA), West Buttress, Salathé, Zodiac, and Magic Mushroom, among others. During a climbing expedition in Kyrgyzstan in 2000, Caldwell, Beth Rodden, and other climbers John Dickey and Jason Smith were kidnapped and held captive by insurgents. Caldwell was ultimately responsible for shoving a lone prisoner down a cliff, which resulted in their escape to government troops. In 2001, Caldwell was sawing away at his left index finger with a table saw when he made an unintentional cut. Doctors were able to repair the damaged piece, but Caldwell decided to have it removed so that it would not interfere with his climbing career going forward.
